Intro to World of Perfumes

Perfume is a complex and fascinating product that has been a part of human culture for centuries. It is an olfactory art form that involves the creation and blending of various aromatic compounds to produce a pleasing and distinctive fragrance. The word "perfume" comes from the Latin "per fumum," meaning "through smoke," highlighting its historical connection to the burning of aromatic substances for ceremonial or religious purposes

History

  • Perfume has a rich history dating back to ancient civilizations, including the Egyptians, Greeks, Romans, and Persians.
  • Early perfumes were often made from natural ingredients like herbs, spices, flowers, and resins.
  • Aroma compounds can be natural or synthetic and contribute to the overall scent profile.

Classification

  1. Eau de Cologne: Light and fresh
  2. Eau de Toilette: Moderately concentrated
  3. Eau de Parfum: More concentrated and longer-lasting
